# The COMMIT Method: Your Guide to Personal Growth

## What is COMMIT?

COMMIT is a structured framework designed to help you achieve clarity, set meaningful goals, and track your personal growth journey. The name stands for five interconnected phases that work together to transform your thoughts into action:

- **C**ontext - Understanding where you are
- **O**bjectives - Defining where you want to go
- **M**indMap - Visualizing connections and possibilities
- **I**deate - Generating creative solutions
- **T**rack - Measuring progress and celebrating wins

Think of COMMIT as your personal growth companion that helps you move from reflection to action, from ideas to achievements.

---

## The Five Phases Explained

### 1. Context (Journal)

**What it is:** Your personal reflection space where you capture thoughts, feelings, and experiences.

**Why it matters:** Before you can move forward, you need to understand where you are right now. Journaling helps you:
- Process emotions and experiences
- Identify patterns in your thoughts and behaviors
- Gain clarity about what's working and what isn't
- Create a record of your journey

**How to use it:**
- Write freely about your day, challenges, or thoughts
- Don't worry about structure—just let your thoughts flow
- Use the AI analysis feature to discover emotional patterns you might not notice
- Review past entries to see how you've grown

**Pro tip:** Regular journaling (even just a few minutes daily) builds self-awareness and helps you make better decisions.

---

### 2. Objectives (Goals & Tasks)

**What it is:** A structured system for breaking down big dreams into manageable steps.

**The Hierarchy:**
- **Vision** - Your long-term aspirations (where you want to be in years)
- **Goals** - Major milestones that move you toward your vision (months to a year)
- **Objectives** - Specific targets that support your goals (weeks to months)
- **Tasks** - Concrete actions you can take today or this week

**Why it matters:** Big goals can feel overwhelming. By breaking them down, you create a clear path forward and make progress visible.

**How to use it:**
1. Start with a **Vision** - What does your ideal future look like?
2. Create **Goals** that align with your vision
3. Break goals into **Objectives** - What needs to happen to achieve each goal?
4. Define **Tasks** - What specific actions will complete each objective?

**Example:**
- **Vision:** "Become a confident public speaker"
- **Goal:** "Deliver 5 presentations this year"
- **Objective:** "Complete a public speaking course"
- **Tasks:** "Research courses," "Enroll in course," "Attend first class"

**Special Features:**

- **Recurring Tasks:** For habits like "Exercise 30 minutes" or "Read for 20 minutes," mark tasks as recurring. You can complete them daily without them disappearing—perfect for building consistent habits.

- **Priority Levels:** Mark tasks as High, Medium, or Low to focus on what matters most

- **Status Tracking:** See at a glance what's Not Started, In Progress, Completed, or On Hold

- **Smart Prioritization:** Drag cards vertically within their columns to set relative importance. The order you create helps you focus on what matters most each day.

- **Family Focus View:** Click any card (Vision, Goal, Objective, or Task) to automatically highlight its entire family tree. This helps you see how your daily tasks connect to your bigger vision. All items remain visible, but your selected family is emphasized for clarity.

- **Quick Navigation:** In the Strategic Map view, click any card title to jump directly to that item in the full Objectives page for detailed editing.

- **Filter by Hierarchy:** Click any Vision, Goal, or Objective card in the Strategic Map to filter and view only related tasks. This helps you focus on one area of your life at a time.

**Pro tip:** Start small. Even tiny tasks completed regularly build momentum toward bigger achievements. Use the family view to remind yourself why each small task matters!

### 3. MindMap

**What it is:** A visual tool that helps you see connections between ideas, problems, and solutions.

**Why it matters:** Sometimes you need to step back and see the big picture. Mind maps help you:
- Break down complex problems into manageable parts
- Discover unexpected connections between ideas
- Visualize relationships between goals, challenges, and opportunities
- Generate new perspectives on old problems

**How to use it:**
1. Enter a problem or challenge you're facing
2. Let AI generate a mind map showing related concepts and connections
3. Explore the visual representation of your thoughts—maps auto-expand to fullscreen for better viewing
4. Click on nodes to create Goals, Objectives, or Tasks directly from insights
5. Save mind maps to revisit and refine later

**New Features:**

- **Visual History:** Your mind map history appears by default beneath the input box as clickable snapshots. Easily revisit past explorations and see your thinking evolve.

- **Fullscreen by Default:** Generated or loaded mind maps automatically expand to fullscreen, giving you maximum space to explore complex ideas.

- **Contextual Exploration:** When generating a new mind map from a selected node, the system maintains context from your previous exploration, ensuring continuity in your thinking.

**Example use cases:**
- "How can I improve my work-life balance?"
- "What steps do I need to start my own business?"
- "How do I overcome my fear of failure?"

**Pro tip:** Use mind maps when you feel stuck or when a problem seems too complex. The visual format often reveals solutions you hadn't considered. Review your mind map history regularly to see patterns in how you approach problems!

### 4. Ideate

**What it is:** Your creative brainstorming space where ideas can grow and evolve.

**Why it matters:** Great achievements start with ideas. The Ideate section helps you:
- Capture thoughts before they slip away
- Expand rough concepts into fully-formed plans
- Connect related ideas to build comprehensive solutions
- Explore different angles and possibilities

**How to use it:**
1. Start with a simple thought or concept (even one sentence works!)
2. Use AI to expand your idea into a detailed concept with:
   - A clear title
   - Expanded content and context
   - Relevant categories and tags
   - Suggestions for next steps
3. Review and refine the AI-generated content
4. Save ideas to your library for future reference
5. Explore connections between your ideas

**Enhanced Editing Experience:**

When editing saved ideas, you now have powerful AI-assisted tools at your fingertips:

- **AI Collaborator Panel:** Access divergent thinking paths, critical analysis, related concepts, and actionable next steps. The panel caches results to avoid redundant processing and includes refresh buttons on each module for when you need fresh perspectives.

- **Smart Text Editing Buttons:**
  - **Enhance:** Select any text to improve its clarity and impact while keeping your voice
  - **Complete:** Place your cursor anywhere and let AI continue writing naturally from that point
  - **Shorten:** Select text to condense it while preserving meaning
  - **Summarize:** Generate a concise summary of your entire idea
  - **Co-create:** Add your cursor after a paragraph and let AI build on your thoughts with new content

- **Convert AI Insights to Action:** In the AI Collaborator, select text from Critical Analysis or Related Concepts and convert it directly into new ideas, tasks, or mind map explorations.

- **Idea Connections:** The app automatically finds closely related ideas from your library based on title and tag similarity, helping you build comprehensive solutions by connecting related thoughts.

- **Next Steps to Tasks:** Convert AI-generated next steps into orphan tasks with one click, ready to be organized in your Objectives hierarchy.

**Smart Library Features:**

- **Collapsible Organization:** The Idea Library auto-collapses when you're viewing AI results, giving you maximum screen space. It expands again after you save changes.

- **Tag-Based Filtering:** Filter your ideas by tags and categories to find exactly what you need when you need it.

- **Mosaic Display:** Ideas are displayed as cards in a responsive grid, making it easy to scan and find what you're looking for.

**Example:**
- **Initial input:** "I want to learn photography"
- **AI expands to:** A full concept covering equipment needs, learning resources, practice strategies, and potential projects
- **You refine with editing tools:** Enhance key sections, complete unfinished thoughts, co-create new approaches
- **Convert insights:** Turn "practice daily" into a recurring task, export related concepts to new ideas

**Pro tip:** Don't judge your initial ideas—just capture them. Use the text editing tools to iteratively refine your thoughts. The Enhance button is perfect for polishing selected text, while Complete helps when you're stuck mid-thought!

### 5. Track

**What it is:** Your customizable progress dashboard that shows how far you've come and what's ahead.

**Why it matters:** What gets measured gets managed. Tracking helps you:
- See your progress over time
- Identify what's working and what needs adjustment
- Stay motivated by celebrating wins
- Spot patterns in your productivity and habits
- Balance creative work with goal achievement

**How to use it:**

The Tracking page now features a flexible, drag-and-drop dashboard where you can arrange widgets to match your workflow:

**Core Tracking Widgets:**

- **Daily Task Trackers:** Minimal 7×4 grids showing your recurring task completion patterns over 4 weeks. Each task has its own bright color, dots fill as you complete tasks, and the display rolls upward weekly (starting Monday, ending Sunday) so you can see your consistency patterns emerge.

- **Stats Overview:** Quick glance at completion rates for Visions, Goals, Objectives, and Tasks with visual progress indicators.

- **Upcoming Deadlines:** See what's coming due soon, organized by urgency to help you prioritize.

**Creative Activity Tracking:**

- **Creative Stats:** Track ideas generated, mind maps created, and total time spent actively using the tool. Balance your strategic planning with creative exploration.

- **Activity Charts:** Visualize your creative activity over time with daily, weekly, or monthly views. See patterns in when you're most productive with ideas and mind mapping.

- **Word Density Map:** A visual cloud of the most frequent words from your journal entries, helping you identify recurring themes in your thoughts.

- **Emotions Over Time:** Track emotional patterns from your journal entries across the last 3 months. See how different emotions trend over time, helping you understand your emotional landscape.

**Customization:**

- **Drag to Rearrange:** Click and drag any widget to move it around the dashboard. Widgets automatically rearrange or swap positions.

- **Persistent Layout:** Your custom arrangement is saved automatically, so your dashboard looks the way you want it every time.

- **Reset Anytime:** Don't like your layout? Reset to the default arrangement with one click.

**Key Metrics Tracked:**

- Task completion rates and streaks
- Creative activity (ideas, mind maps, active time)
- Emotional patterns and word frequency in journaling
- Status distribution across all hierarchy levels
- Recurring habit completion patterns (visualized in daily trackers)

**Pro tip:** Arrange your dashboard to put your most important metrics at the top. If you're focused on building habits, move the Daily Task Trackers to prime position. If you're in a creative phase, prioritize Creative Stats and Activity Charts. Review your tracking data weekly to adjust your approach—if recurring task dots show gaps, consider if the habit is too ambitious and needs breaking down!

## How the Phases Work Together

COMMIT isn't a linear process—it's a cycle that reinforces itself:

1. **Context** helps you understand your current situation
2. **Objectives** turn insights into actionable plans
3. **MindMap** helps you see connections and break down complexity
4. **Ideate** generates creative solutions and alternatives
5. **Track** measures progress and provides feedback
6. **Back to Context** - You reflect on what you've learned and adjust

**Example Flow:**
- You journal about feeling overwhelmed at work (Context)
- You create a goal to improve work-life balance (Objectives)
- You use MindMap to visualize all the factors affecting your balance
- You ideate different strategies for managing time better
- You track your progress on implementing these strategies
- You journal about what's working (back to Context)

## Common Questions

**Q: Do I need to use all five phases?**
A: No! Start with what feels most natural. Many people begin with Context (journaling) and Objectives (goal setting). Add other phases as you become comfortable.

**Q: How often should I use each phase?**
A: It depends on your needs:
- **Context:** Daily or several times per week
- **Objectives:** Weekly review, daily task management
- **MindMap:** When facing complex problems or feeling stuck
- **Ideate:** Whenever inspiration strikes
- **Track:** Daily for tasks, weekly for review

**Q: What if I fall behind on my tasks?**
A: That's okay! Review what happened, adjust your objectives, and break tasks into smaller steps. Progress, not perfection, is the goal.

**Q: Can I use COMMIT for work and personal goals?**
A: Absolutely! Many people use separate Visions for different life areas, or create Goals that span both work and personal growth.

**Q: How do recurring tasks work?**
A: Recurring tasks are perfect for daily habits. Mark a task as recurring, and you can complete it each day without it disappearing. It's great for things like exercise, reading, meditation, or any daily practice. In the Tracking page, you'll see colorful daily tracker grids (7×4) that visualize your completion patterns over 4 weeks, helping you see consistency at a glance.

**Q: How do the AI text editing tools work in Ideate?**
A: When editing an idea, you have five AI-powered buttons:
- **Enhance:** Select text to improve it (replaces selection)
- **Complete:** Place cursor anywhere to continue writing from that point (inserts at cursor)
- **Shorten:** Select text to condense it (replaces selection)
- **Summarize:** Creates a summary of all content (replaces everything)
- **Co-create:** Adds new content after your cursor based on the last paragraph (inserts at cursor)

Each tool is optimized to work within ~500 tokens, ensuring quick, complete responses.

**Q: Can I customize my Tracking dashboard?**
A: Yes! Click and drag any widget to rearrange your dashboard. Your layout is automatically saved. You can reset to default anytime. Organize it to prioritize what matters most to you—daily habit trackers, creative stats, emotional trends, or goal completion rates.

COMMIT works because it:
- **Respects your natural thinking process** - Start anywhere, move between phases as needed
- **Makes progress visible** - See how small actions lead to big changes
- **Provides structure without rigidity** - A framework, not a cage
- **Leverages AI thoughtfully** - Enhances your thinking without replacing it
- **Adapts to you** - Customizable dashboards, smart filtering, and flexible navigation
- **Balances creation and action** - Track creative exploration alongside goal completion
- **Reveals patterns** - Emotional trends, word frequencies, habit consistency, and idea connections
- **Celebrates the journey** - Every entry, task, and idea matters

Remember: Personal growth isn't about perfection—it's about progress. COMMIT gives you the tools to make that progress visible, meaningful, and sustainable. With the latest updates, you have even more control over how you visualize your journey, refine your ideas, and understand your patterns.
